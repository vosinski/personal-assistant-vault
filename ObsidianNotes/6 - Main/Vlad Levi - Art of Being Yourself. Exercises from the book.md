# Autogenic Training – Exercises

## Purpose 1: Training Attention, Concentration, and Memory, and Developing Visual-Spatial Imagination

> [!details] Exercise 1: "Spotlight" (Прожектор)
> This self-suggestion involves imagining a "spotlight" in your head whose beam can illuminate anything with immense power and brightness. When it's focused on something, nothing else exists; everything else is plunged into darkness. This spotlight represents your attention, which you can control as desired, making it ubiquitous and penetrating.  
>  
> **Instructions:**  
> 1. Choose a distant point in your "large circle" of attention (e.g., a far-off light, star, or faint sound).  
> 2. Choose another point near the "small" or "inner" circle (e.g., a finger or the invisible tip of your nose).  
> 3. Perform "swings" of the spotlight from point to point, eyes open or closed.  
> 4. Immerse yourself fully in each point, concentrating as if entering it.  
> 5. Vary the tempo: one second, 10 seconds, one minute, or in sync with your breath.

> [!details] Exercise 2: Continuous Contemplation (Непрерывное созерцание) / "Learning to Look"
> **Instructions:**  
> 6. Sit comfortably.  
> 7. Fix your gaze on any object (coin, matchbox, pencil, finger, cup) for 1–5 minutes.  
> 8. Blink naturally but keep focus on the object.  
> 9. Each time your mind drifts, gently bring attention back.  
> 10. Search for minute details until attention stabilizes.

> [!details] Exercise 3: Rhythmic Contemplation (Ритмичное созерцание) / "Learning to Attend"
> **Instructions:**  
> 11. Choose any object.  
> 12. Focus attention during inhale → close eyes on exhale to “erase” the image.  
> 13. Open eyes on inhale again.  
> 14. Repeat up to 50 times.  
> 15. Experiment with other rhythms (e.g., 5 counts open, 2 counts closed).

> [!details] Exercise 4: Mental Contemplation (Мысленное созерцание) / "Learning to Absorb"
> **Instructions:**  
> 16. Contemplate an object for 3–4 minutes.  
> 17. Close eyes and recreate a mental image of the object.  
> 18. Open eyes, compare with reality.  
> 19. Repeat 5–10 times until your inner image is vivid.  
> 20. Progress from simple objects to complex scenes.

> [!details] Exercise 5: "Inner Videoscope" (Внутренний видеоскоп)
> **Instructions:**  
> 21. Look at one object (e.g., matchbox) for 30 seconds–3 minutes.  
> 22. Then study another (e.g., glass).  
> 23. Mentally place the first object inside the second.  
> 24. Build layered, double, or alternating mental images.  
> 25. This strengthens visual-spatial imagination.

## Purpose 2: General Training of Self-Concentration and Preparation for Self-Suggestions of Warmth

> [!details] Exercise 6: Finger Fixation (Фиксация пальца)
> **Open-eyes variant:** Stare at the fingertip as if seeing it for the first time. Notice warmth/pulsation.  
> **Closed-eyes variant:** Close eyes, place fingertip on support, and concentrate exclusively on the contact point. Visualize impulses, light, warmth, and eventually feel pulse/heat. Expand later to other fingers and body points.

## Purpose 3: Preparation for All Types of Self-Suggestion

> [!details] Exercise 7: Embodying Words and Representations
> **Instructions:** Concentrate on each for 1–10 minutes.  
> - Visualize colors (white, blue, red, green, etc.).  
> - Evoke tastes (lemon, caviar).  
> - Evoke tactile sensations (needle, cotton, feather bed).  
> - Achieve vivid imagery (steam, knife blade, bonfire).  
> - Develop narrative imagery (e.g., beach scene, sun, sea).  
>  
> **Note:** Strong, hallucinatory-like representations are a higher stage of AT.

## Purpose 4: Mental Rest, Renewing Perception, and Creative Mood

> [!details] Exercise 8: "Sliding" 
> **Variants:**  
> - Eyes open, sliding gaze chaotically inside a circle.  
> - Eyes open, unforced wandering.  
> - Eyes closed, attention jumps from one thought to another.  
> This exercise disperses attention, useful for mental rest or sleep induction.

> [!details] Exercise 9: "Rebirth" 
> **Instructions:**  
> 1. Close eyes and self-suggest: "I have forgotten everything."  
> 2. Perform sliding for 2–3 minutes.  
> 3. Open eyes and see surroundings as if anew.  
> 4. Repeat ~5 times, cultivating “infant-like” pure perception.

> [!details] Exercise 10: Pulsation of Attention
> **Instructions:**  
> 1. Choose object/idea (e.g., triangle, math problem).  
> 2. Focus 15–30 seconds intensely.  
> 3. Drop attention into relaxation for 10 seconds.  
> 4. Alternate concentration and relaxation up to 10 times.

# Purpose 5: Releasing Muscle Tension, General Relaxation, and Developing Inner "Muscle Control"

> [!details] Exercise 11: Releasing Tension
> Scan body with attention “spotlight,” release micro-tensions with small movements. Focus especially on common tension zones (forehead, jaws, shoulders, diaphragm, etc.).

> [!details] Exercise 12: Scales of Self-Relaxation
> Systematic relaxation of:  
> - Hands, fingers, forearms, arms.  
> - Legs, hips, knees, feet.  
> - Shoulders, torso, head, jaw, tongue.  
> - Use contrast of tension → relaxation.  
> Goal: total body softness and limpness.

> [!details] Exercise 13: "Smile for Yourself" & Mimetic Exercises
> Alternate tensing and relaxing facial muscles (forehead, eyes, nostrils, lips). Practice a genuine inner smile (like Buddha’s smile) to influence mood and self-control.

> [!details] Exercise 14: Eye Relaxation
> Variants: eyelid tremor control, natural eyelid position, calming self-massage, slow gaze shifts, yogic gazes, distant stare for rest. Eye relaxation induces overall calmness.

> [!details] Exercise 15: "Sliding Pendulum"
> Sequential flex/extend of all joints lightly (head to toe). Effective lying down. Helps quickly shed fatigue.

## Purpose 11: Self-Calming and Deep Relaxation, Facilitating Sleep

## Purpose 12: Developing Self-Confidence and Freedom in Communication

## Purpose 13: General Calming, Organ Harmonization, and Anxiety Relief

> [!details] Exercise 23: "Warmth"
> Self-suggest warmth in hand, then spread to whole body. Combine with free breathing. Useful for vascular health, stress relief, blood pressure regulation.

> [!details] Exercise 24: "Coolness" in the Head
> Self-suggest “forehead is cool.” Useful for calming emotions. If difficult, replace with “face relaxed, warm.”

> [!details] Exercise 25: "With Your Own Hand"
> Place warm hand on body part, suggest transfer of warmth into it. Helps relieve pain, spasms, colics, and discomforts.

## Purpose 14: Deep Self-Relaxation and Background for Self-Suggestions

> [!details] Exercise 26: "Heaviness"
> Self-suggest heaviness in limbs → whole body. Combined with warmth and free breathing, it produces deep relaxation and sleepiness.

# Purpose 15: Overcoming Persistent Negative States & Preparing for Challenges

> [!details] Exercise 27: Pre-Sleep Self-Suggestion (Coué Method)
> Before sleep, monotonously repeat: “This is passing...” until you drift off. Relieves intrusive states and prepares for important events.

> [!details] Exercise 28: Morning Self-Suggestion
> Upon waking, implant mood/goal-setting formulas immediately in receptive half-sleep state. Can also use recordings.

## Purpose 16: Deepening Empathy, Communication Skills, and Personality Restructuring

> [!details] Exercise 29: Empathy and Reincarnation
> Mentally embody objects, people, or roles. Cultivates imagination, empathy, and amplifies self-suggestions.

> [!details] Exercise 30: Inner Double & Stepwise Reincarnations
> Assemble an “ideal self” (Double), inhabit it, and gradually manifest it. Continue evolving with further doubles. Useful for behavior, communication, self-growth.

## Purpose 17: Overcoming Insomnia

> [!details] Exercise 31: How to Trick Insomnia
> Stop *trying* to sleep. Accept wakefulness. Use relaxation, breathing, warmth/heaviness exercises. Changing attitude towards sleeplessness allows natural sleep.

---

## Purpose 18: Enhanced Self-Analysis and Objective Self-Perception

> [!details] Exercise 32: Best Way to Self-Analysis
> Reflect in third person (“he/she” instead of “I”). Helps detach and see yourself objectively, like others do.

---

## Purpose 19: Enhancing Communication Success with Positive Anticipation

> [!details] Exercise 33: "Anticipatory Gratitude"
> Experiment with expecting either negative or positive reactions. Discover how your own expectations shape social interactions. Cultivate inner belief: “I am a source of light, warmth, and happiness.”

---

## Purpose 20: Cultivating Empathy and Balancing Self-Esteem

> [!details] Exercise 34: "Secret Psychologist"
> Redirect attention entirely onto others. Ask countless questions about them internally. Builds true empathy, reduces self-preoccupation, and improves communication.

---

## Purpose 21: Self-Acceptance, Overcoming Hopelessness, and Inner Dignity

> [!details] Exercise 35: Hopeless Situation, or Second Growth
> Recognize that external parameters (height, appearance, intelligence) don’t define worth. Cultivate inner dignity. Others perceive you through your self-attitude. Inner growth is always possible.
