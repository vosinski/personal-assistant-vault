2. I tend to give an imaginary interview after imaiynary success
3. I am dissociated with the future myself. It seems to me that future myself vague and unreal

Possible reasons for third point:
1. Survival Wiring:
If your upbringing made you focus on day-to-day safety (like you mentioned earlier—fear of being robbed if you become wealthy), your brain learned: “The future is dangerous, stay in the now.” That wiring keeps you alive but blocks you from investing energy in future-you.


2. Fear of Collapse:
Subconsciously, you might think: “If I imagine a strong, successful future self, and it doesn’t happen, I’ll be crushed.” So your mind protects you by keeping future-you vague. No vivid dream = no painful disappointment.


3. Neuroscience Gap:
fMRI studies show that when some people imagine their future self, their brain lights up the same way it does when thinking about strangers. So literally, the brain treats “future me” like “some guy far away.” If your mind sees him as a stranger, of course he feels unreal.
